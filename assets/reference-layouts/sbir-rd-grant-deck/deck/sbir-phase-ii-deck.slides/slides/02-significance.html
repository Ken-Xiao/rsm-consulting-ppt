PA-26-014 · Section 2.1 — quoted
"Applications shall demonstrate analytical limit of detection at or below 0.3 ng/mL for the primary biomarker in matched serum and plasma cohorts of at least 200 subjects, with concordance to a reference ELISA exceeding 90%."[2]
Clinical gap
53% of Stage I tumors are missed by current serum ELISA
Existing FDA-cleared assays have LOD ≥ 2.0 ng/mL — above the median Stage-I shedding rate. Helix Phase I demonstrated LOD 0.41 ng/mL in n=72 banked samples.
Economic burden
$26.8B in late-stage treatment costs annually (US)
Stage I → Stage III shift adds ~$94K per patient in lifetime treatment cost. A 10-pt shift to Stage I reduces system cost by ~$2.6B/yr.
Strategic alignment
Cancer Moonshot 2.0 priority area #3
Direct alignment to NCI's published "earlier detection, earlier intervention" priority; also responsive to DOE-NIH joint instrumentation co-funding signal.[3]
