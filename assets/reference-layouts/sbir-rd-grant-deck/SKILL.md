# Win an SBIR Grant like a Phase II awardee

> Every Gantt milestone maps 1:1 to a scored rubric line.

![sbir-rd-grant-deck methodology illustration](https://cdn1.genspark.ai/user-upload-image/slide_agent/v2-catalog-hero/134-sbir-phase2-grant.png)

## Why this skill works

- **The reviewer's checklist is satisfied page-by-page.** Slide headings echo the agency's published rubric verbatim — Significance, Innovation, Approach, Rigor & Feasibility, Investigators & Environment — so the scoring template is the deck's table of contents.
- **The commercialization score crosses 40.** Bottom-up TAM/SAM/SOM, named first customers, a 3-year revenue projection, and a Phase III transition partner with a Letter of Support — the four levers Phase II reviewers empirically score against.
- **You bring your Phase I result, your TRL path, and one transition partner.** Tell the agent the technical achievement Phase I established, the TRL exit criterion, and the named prime / commercial partner; it returns a 16-slide deck ready for the program officer briefing.

## Methodology cheat-sheet

**Rubric Mapping × TRL Progression × Commercialization Discipline** — three pillars from public SBIR Phase II review-instruction PDFs and the *Phase II Brain Trust* feedback loop.

1. **Rubric language on the slide[^2].** Slide headings echo the agency's published criterion language — NIH Simplified Peer Review Framework (Significance / Investigators / Innovation / Approach / Environment reorganized into 3 scored factors).
2. **Milestone = rubric line[^4].** Every Gantt milestone maps 1:1 to a named review criterion. USDA NIFA reviewer instructions make this explicit.
3. **Commercialization plan is venture-grade[^3].** TAM/SAM/SOM (bottom-up), customer segments, channel strategy, named pilot partners, 3-year revenue projections — DOE Phase II commercialization plan spec.
4. **TRL progression with measurable exit criteria[^5].** Phase I exit TRL → Phase II exit TRL → Phase III entry TRL, with the measurement that defines each transition. Navy SBIR Phase II Initial Proposal Instructions.
5. **Named Phase III transition partner[^5][^6].** For DoD / NASA / NIH SBIRs, a named prime / commercialization partner with a Letter of Support is a force-multiplier on the commercialization score.
6. **Risk register with mitigations[^4][^7].** Named risks, scored, with mitigation plans and named owners — not "challenges to be addressed."

## Before / After

### Slide 5 — Phase II Technical Objectives

**Typical PPT template**

> Phase II Goals
> - Continue developing the platform
> - Improve accuracy
> - Scale up the technology
> - Prepare for Phase III

**This skill's rewrite (Slide 05, Technical Objectives — Rubric-Mapped)**

> **TO-1 (Significance):** demonstrate ≤ 0.3 ng/mL LOD for biomarker X in matched serum/plasma cohort (n=240), meeting NIH PA-26-014 Sec 2.1 minimum performance.
>
> **TO-2 (Approach):** achieve ≥ 92% concordance with gold-standard ELISA across 3 reference labs.
>
> **TO-3 (Rigor):** pre-registered analysis plan with α=0.05, β=0.20, n calculated from Phase I variance.

### Slide 12 — Phase III Transition

**Typical PPT template**

> Commercialization
> - We plan to partner with labs
> - Strong commercial potential
> - Multiple potential customers

**This skill's rewrite**

> **Quest Diagnostics signed LoS (attached App C)** committing to a 6-month validation pilot @ $480K, exit-criterion ≥ 90% concordance, transition to Quest's standard send-out menu Q3 2028.
>
> Backstop: LabCorp LoI (App D), parallel pilot.

### Slide 7 — TRL Progression

**Typical PPT template**

> Technology Readiness
> - Phase I demonstrated feasibility
> - Phase II will mature the technology
> - Phase III will commercialize

**This skill's rewrite**

> **TRL 4 (Phase I exit) → TRL 6 (Phase II exit) → TRL 8 (Phase III entry).**
>
> TRL 4 → 5 transition: assay validated in single reference lab (n=80 matched serum samples, ≥85% concordance with gold-standard ELISA). Target month 9.
>
> TRL 5 → 6 transition: multi-site validation across 3 reference labs (n=240, ≥92% concordance). Target month 18.
>
> TRL 6 → 8 transition (Phase III gate): Quest Diagnostics 6-month validation pilot @ ≥90% concordance, transition to Quest's standard send-out menu.

### Slide 13 — Risk Register

**Typical PPT template**

> Risks & Challenges
> - Technical challenges to be addressed
> - Market adoption uncertainty
> - Competitive landscape evolving
> - Regulatory considerations

**This skill's rewrite**

> **Top-5 risks (likelihood × impact × mitigation × owner):**
>
> R-1: cross-lab variability > 8% (likelihood medium, impact high) — mitigation: pre-Phase II 3-site harmonization study (m1-3); owner: Dr Wei.
>
> R-2: Quest pilot delayed > 6 months (likelihood medium, impact medium) — mitigation: LabCorp parallel pilot (App D); owner: VP Commercial.
>
> R-3: FDA CLIA path requires additional validation panel (likelihood low, impact high) — mitigation: pre-IDE meeting requested m4; owner: Regulatory consultant (named).
>
> R-4: key PI departure (likelihood low, impact high) — mitigation: co-I has full protocol access; succession plan in App E.
>
> R-5: reagent supply chain disruption (likelihood low, impact medium) — mitigation: dual-sourced (Thermo + Sigma) since Phase I.

## What this skill produces

A 16-slide, 1920×1080 HTML SBIR Phase II application deck designed for two reading modes: the agency program-officer briefing (read in 25 minutes, with the rubric map on the appendix slide) and the Phase II review panel (read top-to-bottom, scored against the published rubric). Dominant visual mode is **technical schematic + scored-criterion heading + dollar-anchored commercial slide** — not decorative imagery, not stock chemistry clip-art.

The deck enforces three non-negotiable disciplines: (1) **Rubric language on every slide heading** — generic "Significance" without the agency-specific need-statement reference is auto-rejected and rewritten with verbatim PA/NOFO language; (2) **Milestone-to-criterion 1:1 mapping** on the work-plan Gantt — every milestone is colour-coded to a named review criterion; (3) **TRL progression chart** with measurable exit criteria — "TRL will increase" is auto-flagged; the actual measurement that defines each TRL transition must be on the slide.
