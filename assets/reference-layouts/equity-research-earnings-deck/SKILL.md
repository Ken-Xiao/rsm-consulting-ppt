# Write an Earnings Note Deck like a Tier-1 Sell-Side Analyst

> The 30-minute pre-call note that lands in PMs' inboxes before the wire hits.

![equity-research-earnings-deck methodology illustration](https://cdn1.genspark.ai/user-upload-image/slide_agent/v2-catalog-hero/071-morgan-stanley-equity-research.png)

## Why this skill works

- **PMs scan the rating panel, then the bridge — and stop reading.** Rating, PT, and methodology live in a fixed boxout on the cover; the next slide is the beat-vs-consensus bridge.
- **Estimate revisions are explicit.** Slide 09's model-delta table shows old EPS, new EPS, and the delta in basis points — not a "we updated our model" wave.
- **You bring the print + the consensus.** Tell the agent the company, the reported figures, the consensus split, and your rating posture; it returns a deck the morning roadshow can read from.

## Methodology cheat-sheet

**Compliance × Bridge × Rating-Rationale** — three pillars that make a sell-side note both compliant and usable in a 30-minute PM read.

1. **SEC Regulation AC + CFA Standard V[^1]** — analyst certification (the views expressed are the analyst's own) plus the ratings distribution and any material conflicts. The cover footer is required because skipping it is a regulatory issue, not a style issue.
2. **Bridge analysis[^2]** — McKinsey's "Walking the Walk" framing and CFA Institute case material codify the waterfall from reported to consensus: line-item by line-item, with currency and one-time items called out. The deck builds revenue and EPS bridges as the centerpiece of the read.
3. **Rating-rationale boxout[^3]** — bulge-bracket sell-side research shops all converge on the same top-right boxout pattern: rating + PT + return-to-PT + bull/base/bear band + PT methodology footnote. The skill enforces this for cross-firm legibility.
4. **Visible Alpha / Bloomberg consensus discipline[^4]** — every consensus citation includes the source + snapshot timestamp. Stale or undated consensus is the most common compliance flag in earnings notes.
5. **Forward-looking vs. backward-looking discipline** — slide 06 is strictly the print (backward); slide 07 is strictly guidance read-through (forward). Mixing the two is a common analyst-deck failure mode.

## Before / After

### Earnings reaction headline

**Typical PPT template**

> **Q3 FY26 Earnings — Mixed Quarter**
>
> - Revenue $8.2B (above consensus)
> - EPS $2.10 (in line)
> - Solid execution
> - Maintaining Buy rating

**This skill's rewrite (cover sub-headline + slide 04 bridge title)**

> **Reiterate Buy, PT $340 (+18 %) — Q3 revenue beat by $190M on net-new ARR; guide implies a Q4 reset of −2 % vs. consensus; thesis intact.**
>
> *(Cover boxout: Rating Buy · PT $340 · Bull $390 / Bear $250 · Methodology: 25× FY28 P/E discounted 9.5 %)*
>
> *(Slide 04 title: Revenue beat of $190M — $160M from Data Cloud net-new ARR, $50M from FX tailwind, ($20M) from Marketing Cloud renewals.)*

## What this skill produces

A 14-slide, 1920×1080 HTML earnings note deck for a 30-minute internal PM read or a morning sales-call run-through. Dominant visual mode is the **bridge waterfall + estimate-revision table** plus a fixed rating / price-target boxout in the top-right of every slide.

The deck enforces three non-negotiable disciplines: (1) **Compliance cover[^1]** — the analyst certification, ratings distribution, and material-disclosure footnotes anchor the cover, satisfying SEC Regulation AC and CFA Standard V(A); (2) **Bridge from reported to consensus[^2]** — a revenue bridge builds beat/miss from line items, an EPS bridge does the same; both reconcile to the cent; (3) **Rating-rationale boxout[^3]** — bulge-bracket convention: rating + 12-month PT + return-to-PT % + bull/base/bear scenario PT band, with PT methodology footnoted (DCF / multiple / SOTP).
