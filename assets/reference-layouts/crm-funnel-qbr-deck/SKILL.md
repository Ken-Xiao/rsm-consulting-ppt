# Write a CRM Funnel + QBR Deck like a World-Class RevOps Lead

> Tell the CRO which deals to push, pull, or kill — by Monday 09:00.

![crm-funnel-qbr-deck methodology illustration](https://cdn1.genspark.ai/user-upload-image/slide_agent/v2-catalog-hero/070-revops-funnel-qbr.png)

## Why this skill works

- **The CRO opens the deck and immediately knows the call.** Push / Pull / Kill list lives on slide 03 — before stage-by-stage funnels, before forecast.
- **Stage definitions stop sliding.** The deck locks the CRM stage labels and exit criteria on slide 02, so "Stage 3 conversion" means the same thing in Q3 and Q4.
- **You bring a CRM export + a quarter.** Tell the agent the CRM (HubSpot / Salesforce), the closed-won/lost period, and the rep cohort; it returns a deck that survives the QBR.

## Methodology cheat-sheet

**Stage Lock-in × Pipeline Waterfall × Forecast Accuracy** — three pillars that turn a CRM export into a QBR the CRO can actually trust.

1. **CRM Stage Lock-in** — RevOps's job is to make "Stage 3" mean the same thing on Tuesday and Thursday. The deck makes the stage taxonomy a slide, not an assumption.
2. **MEDDIC vs. SPICED[^1][^2]** — MEDDIC (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion) for enterprise; SPICED (Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision) for fast-moving / PLG motions. The qualification slide uses whichever the team has standardised on.
3. **Pipeline Waterfall[^3]** — the canonical RevOps slide: opening pipeline + new pipeline created + slippage in + slippage out + closed-won + closed-lost = ending pipeline. Every quarter, every motion.
4. **Forecast accuracy framework[^4]** — Gartner CSO research codifies commit / most-likely / best-case bands and linearity tracking; the deck reports last 4 quarters of accuracy to keep the number honest.
5. **Salesforce State of Sales reference[^5]** — used as benchmark overlays for stage-by-stage conversion and quota attainment so the deck doesn't celebrate average performance.

## Before / After

### Quarterly performance slide

**Typical PPT template**

> **Q3 Performance Highlights**
>
> - Pipeline grew 25 %
> - Closed-won was $42M
> - Strong quarter for the team
> - On track for Q4

**This skill's rewrite (slide 03, Push / Pull / Kill)**

> **3 calls for the next 10 days:**
>
> **Push** · Acme ($1.8M ACV) — champion confirmed, legal closing this week, push CFO call by Thu.
> **Pull** · Globex ($900k) — no economic buyer named in 21 days; pull back to Stage 2 until Identified.
> **Kill** · Initech ($600k) — closed-lost 2 prior quarters, no exec sponsor; remove from forecast and reassign rep capacity.

## What this skill produces

A 16-slide, 1920×1080 HTML deck for a 60-90 minute Quarterly Business Review, also usable as a monthly forecast call deck (cut to 10 slides). Dominant visual mode is the **funnel + waterfall pair** plus rep-level small-multiples on activity and conversion.

The deck enforces three non-negotiable disciplines: (1) **Stage definition lock-in** — slide 02 documents the CRM stage IDs, names, and exit criteria, so every downstream conversion number ties to one named stage; (2) **Pipeline Waterfall pattern[^3]** — opening pipeline + new business + slippage + push + close = ending pipeline, drawn explicitly so leadership sees where the quarter actually moved; (3) **Forecast accuracy disclosed[^4]** — Gartner's CSO framework: commit / best-case / pipeline coverage / linearity, with last-quarter's actual accuracy band on the same slide. Qualification scoring uses MEDDIC[^1] or SPICED[^2] depending on the team's sales motion.
