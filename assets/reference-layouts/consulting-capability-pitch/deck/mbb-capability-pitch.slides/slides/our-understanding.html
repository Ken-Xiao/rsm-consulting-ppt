03  ·  Our Understanding of Your Situation
Three priorities the Halsey board put on the table this quarter.
Drawn from your 2025 Annual Report (pp. 14–22), the 18 March investor call transcript, and a 90-minute conversation with CFO M. Hartmann on 6 May. Phrasing is yours; we have re-ordered only by financial materiality.
Priority 01
Reverse three quarters of EMEA margin compression.
Specialty-chemicals segment EBITDA margin fell from 18.4% (Q2 '25) to 14.1% (Q1 '26). Coatings sub-line has carried the loss; Halsey's own analysis attributes ~60% to channel-mix drift and ~40% to under-priced renewals.
Source — Q1 2026 earnings call
"We will not accept a fourth quarter of margin compression in coatings." — CFO M. Hartmann
Priority 02
Rebuild the commercial muscle in Tier-2 city coverage.
The 2024 distributor consolidation left 23 second-tier cities with single-source coverage. Win-rates in those geographies have dropped from 41% to 29% year-over-year, and your top three competitors have hired ~80 reps into those exact cities.
Source — Annual Report 2025, p. 18
"Coverage gaps are now our single biggest growth headwind." — CCO S. Larsson, 18 March
Priority 03
Have something the IPO roadshow can defend on growth.
A credible 14-week working plan that produces 2–3 quantified quick wins by Q3, plus a defensible four-quarter trajectory the Halsey IR team can present to anchor investors in the H1 2027 listing window.
Source — CFO conversation, 6 May
"Anchor investors want numbers they can underwrite, not aspirations." — CFO M. Hartmann
