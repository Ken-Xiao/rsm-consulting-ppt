09  ·  Methodology
Hypothesis-first. Customer-by-customer. Decision-gated.
Three operating disciplines we apply to every revenue-growth engagement. Each is borrowed from a body of work the SteerCo can read on its own; none is proprietary jargon.
01
Hypothesis-driven, Pyramid-Principle written.
Every deliverable opens with the finding, then the supporting evidence, then the implication. We write slide titles as findings, not topics. Lifted from Minto and operationalized in The McKinsey Engagement (Rasiel).
Reference — Minto, Pyramid Principle  ·  Rasiel, McKinsey Engagement [^7][^8]
02
Customer-by-customer evidence beats segment-level theory.
Halsey's renewal book has 217 contracts. Margin recovery is decided by the 38 underwater ones, not by an industry index. The model is built bottom-up — every quick win is named, owned, and underwriteable by week 6.
Reference — Sequoia & Maister on proof-stack discipline [^2]
03
Decision-gated cadence — every Tuesday, a binary go / no-go.
No drift. Workplan in 14 weeks has 6 decision gates (slide 11). At each gate the SteerCo confirms one of three states: continue current hypothesis, pivot to alternate hypothesis, or stop the workstream. The partner is in the room every Tuesday — not flying in for week 1 and week 12.
