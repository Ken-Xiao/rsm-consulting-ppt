# Diagnose a Client like a Tier-1 Implementation Lead

> 12 opportunities, dollar-sized, ranked on a 2×2 — in one week.

![consulting-diagnostic-audit-deck methodology illustration](https://cdn1.genspark.ai/user-upload-image/slide_agent/v2-catalog-hero/135-mckinsey-diagnostic-audit.png)

## Why this skill works

- **The Steering Committee chair reads slide 2 and gets the answer.** Minto's Situation-Complication-Resolution is one paragraph and one chart — the rest of the deck supports it, never re-explains it.
- **The phase-2 statement of work signs on Friday.** Twelve opportunities, dollar-sized with confidence levels, plotted on an effort-vs-impact 2×2 with the top-5 named — the client sees the next 6 months as math, not a pitch.
- **You bring interview notes, a benchmark, and an opportunity list.** Tell the agent the baseline KPIs, the comp benchmark, and the raw opportunity register; it returns an 18-slide partner-review-grade deck with RAPID, quick-wins, and a phase-2 roadmap.

## Methodology cheat-sheet

**Minto × Performance Improvement × RAPID** — three pillars from MBB tier-1 strategy diagnostic playbooks, used together in every partner-grade week-1 readout.

1. **Pyramid principle, one-page executive answer first[^6].** Slide 2 is the Situation-Complication-Resolution executive answer; everything else supports it. Barbara Minto via the canonical tier-1 strategy-consulting training canon.
2. **Benchmark before opportunity[^7].** No improvement opportunity is named without a baseline number + internal-best-practice or external-comp benchmark. The canonical holistic benchmarking approach.
3. **Dollar-size every opportunity[^2][^7].** Each opportunity has an estimated annualized $ impact, confidence level (low/medium/high), and one named source. The published *Operational Performance Improvement in Industrial Companies* research names the 10 levers.
4. **Effort-vs-impact 2×2 ranks the top 5[^1][^2].** Standard tier-1 strategy-consulting prioritization tool. Top-5 named explicitly; quick-wins quadrant highlighted.
5. **RAPID for organizational root causes[^3].** Where the diagnostic surfaces a decision-rights problem, the slide expresses it in RAPID terms — Recommend / Agree / Perform / Input / Decide. Rogers & Blenko's *Who Has the D?* via the published RAPID framework.
6. **Implementation roadmap promises Results Delivery rigor[^5].** The closing slides commit to named workstreams, named owners, and a 90-day quick-win pulse — Bain's named change-management practice.

## Before / After

### Slide 5 — Top-5 Improvement Opportunities

**Typical PPT template**

> Areas of Opportunity
> - Procurement efficiency
> - SG&A optimization
> - Pricing improvements
> - Working capital management
> - Network rationalization

**This skill's rewrite (Slide 05, Top-5 Improvement Opportunities)**

> **OP-1 (Procurement, indirect spend re-tender) — $14.2M annualized, high confidence.**
>
> **OP-2 (Pricing, list-to-pocket leakage) — $9.8M annualized, medium confidence.**
>
> **OP-3 (SG&A, span-of-control normalization to 7) — $7.1M annualized, high confidence.**
>
> **OP-4 (Working capital, DSO 62→48) — $6.4M one-time + $0.9M run-rate.**
>
> **OP-5 (Footprint, two Tier-3 DC consolidation) — $4.7M annualized, medium confidence.**

### Slide 12 — Organizational Root Cause (RAPID)

**Typical PPT template**

> Organizational Challenges
> - Decisions take too long
> - Siloed ownership
> - Lack of clarity on roles
> - Need to align stakeholders

**This skill's rewrite**

> **Pricing-exception approval is broken: 4 Recommenders (Sales, Finance, Product, Legal), 3 Agreers, no clear Decider — currently defaulting to CEO for ~190 cases/quarter.**
>
> RAPID redesign: Sales VP = Recommend; CFO = Decide; Product + Legal = Input only (not Agree). Expected: cycle 9 days → 36 hours.

### Slide 2 — Executive Answer (Minto)

**Typical PPT template**

> Executive Summary
> - The client faces significant challenges
> - There are multiple improvement opportunities
> - We recommend a phased approach
> - Phase 2 will deliver results

**This skill's rewrite**

> **Situation:** the client's EBITDA margin has eroded from 14.2% (FY24) to 9.8% (FY26 LTM), trailing the industrial-peer median by 380 bps despite stable revenue.
>
> **Complication:** internal benchmarking attributes the gap to indirect-spend leakage, pricing-exception drift, and SG&A span inflation — not volume or product mix.
>
> **Resolution:** a 6-month phase-2 transformation can recover $42M annualized EBITDA (28% margin uplift) across 5 named workstreams, with $11M of quick-wins delivered by day 90.
>
> *(Supporting chart: 8-quarter EBITDA bridge, with the 380-bp peer gap and the 28% recovery target highlighted.)*

### Slide 6 — Effort-vs-Impact 2×2

**Typical PPT template**

> Prioritization
> - High-priority items
> - Medium-priority items
> - Low-priority items
> - Quick wins

**This skill's rewrite**

> **All 14 opportunities plotted on effort (x-axis) × impact (y-axis); dot-size = annualized $ impact.**
>
> Quick-wins quadrant (low effort, high impact): OP-1 ($14.2M), OP-3 ($7.1M), OP-4 ($6.4M run-rate).
>
> Big-bets quadrant (high effort, high impact): OP-2 ($9.8M), OP-5 ($4.7M).
>
> Below-line (de-prioritized for phase 2): OP-9 ($1.4M, low confidence), OP-11 ($0.9M, dependency risk), OP-13 ($0.6M).
>
> *Top-5 explicitly named; total $-impact at risk: $42.2M annualized.*

## What this skill produces

An 18-slide, 1920×1080 HTML diagnostic deck designed for two simultaneous reading modes: the **client Steering Committee readout** (Minto answer in 5 minutes, top-5 opportunities in 10 minutes, phase-2 ask in 3 minutes) and the **internal partner review** (intellectual-honesty audit of the dollar-sizing methodology, the benchmark sources, and the RAPID diagnosis). Dominant visual mode is **dollar-sized 2×2 dot chart + RAPID grid + named lever slides** with McKinsey-style exhibit-number / source-line footers on every slide.

The deck enforces three non-negotiable disciplines: (1) **Minto executive answer on slide 2** — Situation / Complication / Resolution in one paragraph plus one supporting chart, no exceptions; (2) **Dollar-sized opportunities on the 2×2** — each opportunity is a labelled dot, dot-size proportional to $ impact, position = effort × impact, top-5 explicitly named, quick-wins quadrant called out; (3) **RAPID for organizational root causes** — wherever the diagnostic surfaces a decision-rights problem, the slide expresses it in RAPID terms (Recommend / Agree / Perform / Input / Decide), not in narrative prose[^3].
